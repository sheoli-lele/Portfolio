## About Me

I garnered an interest in philosophy of technology early in college, which was the gateway that led me to pursue a degree in it. To me, the most poignant topics were the possibility of machine consciousness, the changing of the self, and The Extended Mind Hypothesis. All of these topics have gained relevance in 2026, with the rapid release of LLM tools for education, industry, and public usage. Here(https://docs.google.com/document/d/1gkfU6plE-Turb3ora7SNuGuiURDsMivAbejEOCOXjuc/edit?tab=t.0) is a piece I wrote at the time.

As my interest in ethics grew, I wrote increasingly complex pieces, engaging with the latest works in the field of Nonconsequentialist Ethics. Here(https://docs.google.com/document/d/1f2Ba6Y1oAbFGm0_mko_5fcvpt4elzpZY4VkE1jO0PVs/edit?tab=t.0) is one of those pieces.

## Ethics Bowl: Regional and National Champion

My two-person team won the IEB Ethics Bowl Regional Championship in early November 2025, coming out at the top of 20+ teams, and beating the University of Pennsylvania and Yale University teams in the final two rounds. Among the cases we were asked to research and discuss were the present and future rights of non-biological (digital) entities.

Read about our win(https://www.prindleinstitute.org/news/iebonline-25-recap/).

I was also in charge of research and prep for my team's sub-group who won the Association for Practical and Professional Ethics (APPE) National Championship. Read about my team's win(https://www.appe-ethics.org/appe-ieb-national-competition/).

## Technical Background

My interest and experience in critical thinking and ethical reasoning is only half the story; I am also a student of Data Science and Statistics. I think of this technical background as foundational to my ethics and critical analysis work.

In the past year, I learned the ins and outs of High-Performance Computing (HPC) using Python and Bash, and can now design and execute complex data pipelines on high-performance clusters. Along with three peers, I built a machine learning pipeline on an HPC cluster that predicts a pilot's cognitive state (focused, distracted, startled, etc.) from real-time physiological data—EEG, heart rate, breathing, and skin conductance. I handled data cleaning, feature engineering, and model optimization across 17 pilots. See the repository for yourself (https://github.com/vkalithkar/Flight-Crew-Analytics).

## Writing on AI Policy & Ethics

Over the past few months, I have begun to record my ideas on the policy and ethical implications of AI tools in regular blog posts. Each piece is thoroughly researched and put out as a starting point for discussion.

#### The Alignment Problem

My first piece is about the Alignment Problem. I introduce the problem—which many AI skeptics do not realize is the term capturing one of their biggest worries about the technology—and offer a unique perspective on it. Read the piece (https://substack.com/home/post/p-192360219).

#### The Functionalist Problem

My second post argues that despite our lack of understanding about complex neural networks' internal mechanisms (which we call "the black box problem"), there are risks associated with our putting off this pursuit of a deeper understanding, including alignment problems. I review Alan Turing's paper that introduced what is known as the "Imitation Game," a game built on an assumption (of "functionalism") that I reject. Read this piece (Read the piece).

## Teaching 

I have taken up a part-time role teaching mathematics (mostly trigonometry, calculus, and statistics). This is an orthodox thing to include in a portfolio—I'm including it as an opportunity to discuss the value I get from teaching material to those struggling. Learning the fundamentals of material I was shown 5–6 years ago has closed many fundamental gaps in my understanding and, more importantly, raised new questions (e.g. How does the "+C" matter so much in indicating that antiderivatives form a family of parallel curves, which by definition have different y-intercepts?).
